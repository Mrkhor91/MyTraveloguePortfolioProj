My Travelogue: Out in the Wild

In this section of my travelogue, I will share stories of my trips to some of the amazing hidden gems in my country. From serene lakes nestled in secluded valleys to charming villages untouched by time, each destination offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. These are places that often escape the typical travel itineraries, yet they hold a special charm and allure that make them unforgettable. Join me as I uncover these lesser-known treasures, offering a glimpse into the breathtaking landscapes and vibrant traditions that make my country so extraordinary. Whether it's a tranquil beach, a mystical forest, or a historic town, these hidden gems promise to captivate your heart and inspire your wanderlust

The beautiful Kalam Valley

Kalam Valley, nestled in the Swat district, is a breathtakingly beautiful region characterized by its lush green meadows, dense forests, and the pristine Swat River that flows through it. The valley is surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks, offering a serene and picturesque landscape that captivates visitors with its tranquil charm and natural splendor.
I first visited it back in 2015 with my group. To reach this place we had to drive for 9 hours from Islamabad. The road from Bahrian to Kalam was in bad shape earlier but now its being recarpeted so traveling is very much eased up. From Kalam we traveled towards North-East to reach Ushu which is just a beautiful dense forest. From there, we traveled for one hour further to reach magnificient Mahodand lake, which is quite special as it looks more like a flowing stream than a lake.

Summer 2015

Taobat- Last Village of AJK

Taobat, the last village of Azad Kashmir in Pakistan, is a hidden gem that boasts unparalleled natural beauty. Nestled amidst the Himalayas, this picturesque village is surrounded by lush green valleys, dense forests, and clear, flowing streams. In summer, the landscape comes alive with vibrant colors, offering a mesmerizing contrast against the backdrop of towering peaks. Captured from a drone in 2015, the aerial view of Taobat reveals its serene and untouched charm, with traditional wooden houses dotting the verdant terrain. This tranquil haven is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. My first trip to this place was just full of adventures as we traveled from central punjab to this place and then went back within a span of weekend

Summer 2015

Mesmerizing Ratti Gali Lake

Ratti Gali Lake, located in the mesmerizing Neelum Valley, is a sight to behold with its crystal-clear blue waters reflecting the surrounding snow-capped peaks. The lush green meadows adorned with vibrant wildflowers create a stunning contrast against the serene lake, making it a perfect haven for nature enthusiasts.
I first visited this lake in summer 2016 and have gone twice after that, but everytime i visit this place it brings amazing memories. To reach this lake i had to drive for 7-8 hrs from Islamabad and then take a thrilling two hour jeep ride from Dowarian in Neelum Valley. There is a camping area at base camp where all the facilities are available.

Summer '16

Winter Paradise - Naltar Resort

Naltar Ski Resort, situated in the picturesque Gilgit district of Pakistan, is a winter paradise renowned for its stunning alpine scenery and excellent skiing conditions. Nestled amidst snow-capped peaks and dense pine forests, the resort offers a serene yet thrilling experience for ski enthusiasts. The well-maintained slopes cater to both beginners and experienced skiers, providing an exhilarating descent against the backdrop of breathtaking mountain views.
Being a part of Skiing team of my organization i have visited this place many times to participate in local competetions.

Winter '17

Hidden Gem - Chitta Khatta Lake

Chitta Khatta Lake, located in the enchanting Upper Neelum Valley of Kashmir, is a hidden gem known for its breathtaking beauty and serene surroundings. This pristine alpine lake, surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks, offers a stunning contrast with its crystal-clear waters reflecting the sky and mountains.
I went on this trip with four bestfriends. Initially we drove all the way to Kel and then took a jeep ride to Shounter. And from there it was a challenging 7-8 hour hike, making it a rewarding destination for avid trekkers. The untouched and tranquil beauty of Chitta Khatta Lake, makes it a must-visit for those seeking solitude and natural splendor in the heart of Kashmir.

Summer '17

Frozen Saif-ul-Malook

Saif-ul-Malook Lake, situated at an elevation of 3,224 meters above sea level in the enchanting Naran Valley, stands as one of the most picturesque destinations in Pakistan. I had been to this lake before in summers but i did not like it back then. But in winters this breathtaking lake transforms into a frozen wonderland. The serene, ice-covered surface of the lake mirrors the surrounding snow-clad peaks, creating a mesmerizing and tranquil landscape and it is also a good place for ice-skating as well. Reaching the top i felt captivated by the crystal-clear reflections and the stark, pristine beauty that surrounds it.
We embarked on a journey to this lake in November, little did we know that all the hotels in Naran close down in Early November and road to the Naran gets frosty at night. We survived falling off the road couple of times but nonetheless it was a memorable trip.

Winter '18

Heaven On Earth - Minimerg

Rainbow Lake in Minimerg is a tranquil and breathtakingly beautiful spot nestled in the Astore Valley of Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan. Surrounded by towering, rugged mountains partially cloaked in snow, the lake's crystal-clear waters reflect the stunning landscape, creating a serene and picturesque scene. The vibrant colors of the surrounding meadows and the occasional patches of wildflowers add to the enchanting charm of this hidden gem.
I visited this lake back in 2019 with two of my very close friends. At first we tried to use Babusar route unaware of the fact that it was still covered with snow. Then we had to spend a night in Kaghan before proceeding to Astore via KKH. It takes 17 hrs to travel to Astore from Abbotabad and from Astore it takes 5-6 hours on jeep to reach this piece of Heaven. I have been to almost all the major tourist destinations in Pakistan but there is nothing more greener and peaceful as this small town.

Summer'19

Katora Lake with the boys

Katora Lake is an alpine glacial lake located in the upper reaches of the Jahaz Banda, Kumrat Valley within the Upper Dir District of the KPK. It was named Katora meaning "bowl"after the lakes resemblance to a bowl shape. We started our journey from Islamabad at night and reached Thal in the morning, from where we took a jeep to Taki Banda. After reaching Taki Banda, we were all tired but we did not want to waste our time there so we started trek to Jahaz Banda straigh away. It was a 2-3 hour trek to Jahaz Banda which is a beautiful meadow. We camped the night there and started our journey to Katora lake in the morning, which was much easier then previous hike. We spent the day there and then moved back to Islamabad in the night.

Summer'19

Lone Wolf

Marsur Rock Trek in Hussainabad is an amazing picturesque spot. There are only two protruding rock structures in the world which give such scenic view of the valley, one is in Norway with the name of Trolltunga and other is in Skardu. One more unique thing about this place is that there is no proper trek leading to this point, it is totally out in the wild without any navigational points
Hussainabad, situated just after Skardu en route to Khaplu, serves as the starting point for this exhilarating trek. The journey commences with a scenic drive up a winding road on the upper side of the town, followed by an hour-long hike through lush greenery. Subsequently, you’ll encounter a steeper trail, taking around 2 hours to conquer if you are physically fit. As you continue, the path gradually levels out, leading you to a unique and extensive sloping stone.
